                <h3>How 2000s Fashion is Making a Comeback</h3>
                <p> The 2000s (Y2K) fashion is making a huge comeback, largely fueled by Gen Z's nostalgia and the cyclical nature of trends. Key items like low-rise jeans, cropped baby tees, velour tracksuits, and mini shoulder bags are dominating. This revival is powered by social media platforms like TikTok, allowing for a fresh, bold, and playful reinterpretation of the era’s maximalist and experimental aesthetic.</p> 
                <br>
                <hr>

                
                <h3>2000s Red Carpet Moments That Defined the Decade</h3>
                <p> The 2000s red carpet was defined by bold, headline-making fashion. Iconic moments include Jennifer Lopez’s plunging green Versace gown (which famously led to the creation of Google Images), Britney and Justin's matching all-denim ensembles, and Bjork's unforgettable swan dress. These unapologetically showy and experimental looks cemented the decade’s maximalist aesthetic, prioritizing shock value and celebrity influence.</p>
                <br>
                <hr>
                
                <h3>Celebrity Style Icons of the 2000s</h3>
                <p> The 2000s were dominated by celebrity-driven style, led by Paris Hilton and Britney Spears, who popularized the iconic Juicy Couture tracksuit and low-rise denim. Jennifer Lopez brought 'bling' and vibrant glamour, while Sarah Jessica Parker (as Carrie Bradshaw) championed high-fashion risks. Their casual yet extravagant looks set the playful, over-the-top, and logo-heavy tone for the entire Y2K era.</p>
